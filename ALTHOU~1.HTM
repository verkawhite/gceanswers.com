Question.Although your school provides devices for all students, many of the students in your organization do not have Internet access at home. There is concern that students will then have unequal access to Google Workspace for Education. How can you enable students to work on Google files offline? Match the solution to the correct platform.

Explanation.If students do not have access to the internet at home, they may not be able to use Google Workspace for Education to complete their assignments. One solution to this problem is to enable offline access for Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drawings. This can be done through the Chrome browser on desktops and laptops. By enabling offline access, students can download their Google files onto their devices and work on them even when they are not connected to the internet. Once they regain internet access, their changes will automatically sync with the online version of the file.

For students using Android and iOS devices, enabling offline access can be done through the Drive app. By enabling offline access, students can download their Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides files onto their mobile devices and work on them without an internet connection. Once they regain internet access, their changes will automatically sync with the online version of the file.

By enabling offline access for Google files, students who do not have internet access at home can still work on their assignments and have equal access to Google Workspace for Education. This solution provides a practical workaround for students who may not have reliable internet access at home.

Answer.A correct answer is "Enable offline access in the Chrome browser to edit Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drawings. - Desktops and Laptops" and "Enable offline access in the Drive app to edit Docs, Sheets, and Slides. - Android and iOS".
